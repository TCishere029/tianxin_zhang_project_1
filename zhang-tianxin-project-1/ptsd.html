PTSD

Children's Mental Health Crisis Line: 651-266-7878

Adult Mental Health Crisis Line: 651-266-7900

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) is a psychiatric condition triggered by witnessing or experiencing a traumatic event, such as warfare, natural disasters, physical or sexual assault, or other life-threatening incidents. Individuals with PTSD often grapple with distressing memories, flashbacks, nightmares, and heightened emotional reactions, which can severely impede daily functioning and relationships. While the exact mechanisms underlying PTSD are still being studied, a combination of genetic, environmental, and psychological factors are believed to contribute to its development. Early intervention and various therapeutic strategies, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy, can offer relief to many affected individuals.
